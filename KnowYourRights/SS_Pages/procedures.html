Procedures & Rights

In Western Australia, the police may stop and search you, your house or your vehicle randomly if they believe you are connected to a crime or suspicious activity. Police don't require search warrants or your consent to perform this search.

Anytime before or during a stop and search:

  • You may ask police why they are performing the search.
  • When asked, the police must outline reasons for the search.
  • Police may use reasonable force to conduct the search.
  • If you feel the police have used unnecessary force to search, you may seek legal advice or file a complaint to police.
